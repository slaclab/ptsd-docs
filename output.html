2020 Restart - Safety, JSA Information, Planning

Introduction/Overview

The restart of the extended experimental facilities at SLAC requires the careful design and scheduling of resources to meet the requirements of SLAC management, the county and Dept. of Energy. The nature of the Precision Timing systems is such that we have multiple “sinks” in terms of the documentation and planning that needs to happen to insure that our systems are read to go when needed for operations.

The purpose of this page is to provide a somewhat ad-hoc and intentionally temporary area to collect pointers to where the various tools and documents we need are kept and reviewed, without duplicating copies. Any member of the PTSD should be able to use this area to:

  1. Locate the source materials for work that they are involved in
  2. Locate the area and people involved in approving and managing work in their area
  3. Track the status of JSAs for PTSD work

The general flow of information for review and planning of work follows:

Taken from the SLAC all-hands, stages of operational recovery fall into the following categories:

  1. Stage 0 : Initial Phase COVID Research and Minimal Function Maintenance of Facility
  2. Stage 1 : Restart of Experimental and Core Science Programs - This represents the bulk of the Precision Timing System Recovery
  3. Stage 2 : Restoration of functions closely related to moving stage 1 work beyond restart (shops and labs)
  4. Stage 3 : Most of lab is back, though people remain telecommuting where feasible
  5. Stage 4 : General Recovery of Lab function

Not to be confused with the Stages, the types of work carried out in these phases falls into the following levels:
